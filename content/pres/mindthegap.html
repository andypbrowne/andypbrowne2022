Mind the Gap

Mind what gap?
The gap is the learning curve between graphic and web design. This lecture and workshop seeks to provide some tools, exercises, and approaches to mind the gap. We will discuss a few principles that are fundamental to all design practice but especially important to design for the web: web design is semantic; user-centered; empathic; invisible, and content-out.

Join Andrew Paul Browne, Artist/Designer ('11) for a presentation and discussion Thursday, April 23 at the beginning of Professional Practice in Room 906 | The Art Institute of New York City

Mind the Gap

MIND THE GAP

The Gap is a learning curve between graphic and web design. Filling that gap will require some tools, experimentation & play and a content-out approach to design in general.

SOME TOOLS

Development tools, available in all browsers allow for debugging, style/ layout adjustments, and learning. A multitude of design elements like webfonts and the grid can be manipulated in this way.

EXPERIMENTATION & PLAY

Both introductory and seasoned practice in web design require sketching ideas, making use of a defined process, and adoption of a prototyping method.

CONTENT-OUT BASED APPROACH

In order to communicate information (the primary purpose of the web) and create user-centered solutions we make use of carefully set typography, clear hierarchy, and whitespace.
